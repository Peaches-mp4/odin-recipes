Watermelon Mojito

Description

This watermelon mojito recipe is perfect for summertime parties, BBQs, birthdays, and girls' nights. Is there anything more summery than a fresh, juicy watermelon? This drink is refreshing, delicious, and super easy to make on a hot day â€” not to mention, it looks beautiful in your glass! So why not kick back, relax, and enjoy a tall glass of this mouthwatering cocktail and watch as your stresses just melt away? Garnish with lime and watermelon slices, plus fresh mint if desired.

Ingredients

  • 55 ml of watermelon juice (try to look for a ripe and in-season watermelon, since unripe melons will not have nearly as much flavor)
  • 10-12 mint leaves (fresh mint if possible)
  • 30 ml of lime juice (freshly squeezed is best)
  • 55 ml rum
  • 1 tbsp of sugar (a traditional mojito uses cane sugar instead of simple syrup)
  • 28 ml of seltzer

Steps

  1. Start by cutting your watermelon into chunks. You'll need about double the volume of chunks to the amount of juice you want to make, so 2 cups of chunks will make about a cup of juice once it's strained.
  2. Once you have your watermelon juice ready, get started making the drink by adding your fresh mint leaves to the bottom of a cocktail shaker.
  3. Then, add in the granulated sugar, and stir them together lightly with a bar spoon. The stirring action will release the natural oils in the mint without crushing it or releasing any bitterness.
  4. Add in the lime juice, and stir to start dissolving the sugar. Then, add in the watermelon juice.
  5. Add the rum to the serving glass, and then fill the glass with ice. Give it a quick stir to combine everything.
  6. Top off the drink with seltzer. Garnish the drink with a watermelon slice and a sprig of fresh mint before serving.
  7. Enjoy!
