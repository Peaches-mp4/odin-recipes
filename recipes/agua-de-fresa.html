Mexican Strawberry Water (Agua de Fresa)

Description

Agua de fresa is a delightful beverage made by blending fresh strawberries, water, sugar, and occasionally milk.

Aguas frescas (or "fresh waters") are non-alcoholic iced drinks served by street vendors in Mexico and Central America. They are typically made by combining fresh fruits, grains, seeds, herbs, spices, and sweeteners.

According to the Mexican legends, Aztecs would add ice from the dormant volcanoes Popocatépetl and Iztaccíhuatl to make this drink cold!
